Timeline of Mariano Rivera Career in Professional Baseball:

  • 1995:Rivera was called up to the major league on May 16, 1995. Rivera finished his first major league season with a 5-3 record and a 5.51 ERA in ten starts and nine relief outings.
  • 1996:Starting the 1996 season, Rivera served primarily as a setup pitcher, typically pitching in the seventh and eighth innings. Rivera finished the regular season with an 2.09 ERA in 107 2/3 innings pitched and set a Yankees single-season record for strikeouts by a reliever which was 130
  • 1997:Rivera transition from the setup man to closer but blew three of his first six save opportunities. However, Rivera made his first All-Star with 27 saves and a 1.96 ERA at the midseason break and ending the season with 43 saves in 52 opportunities with a 1.88 ERA.
  • 1998:Rivera saved 36 games in 41 opportunities and had a 1.91 ERA in the regular season. During the postseason, he pitched 13 1/3 scoreless innings and saved six games. He also recorded the final out in game 4 of the 1998 World Series which clinched the New York Yankees Championship.
  • 1999:Rivera was selected to the All-Star team for the second in his career and his famous entrance was born which was "Enter Sandman". Rivera ended the season with 45 saves in 49 opportunities with a 1.83 ERA
  • 2000:Rivera was selected as an All-Star for the the third time and ended with a 2.85 ERA having 36 saves in 41 opportunities. During the postseason, Rivera saved six games allowed three earned runs in 15 2/3 innings pitched and broke a major league record with the most saves in postseason history earning the name "infallible weapon" due his reputation as an exceptional postseason performer.
  • 2001:Before the 2001 season, Rivera signed a four-year $39.99 million deal with the New York Yankees, marking the first long-term contract of his career. Rivera was selected to his third All-Star game and finished the season with a 2.34 ERA with 50 saves in 57 opportunities.
  • 2002-2005:Rivera next three seasons was injury plagued limiting his playing time.
  • 2006:Rivera achieved the milestone of becoming the fourth MLB pitcher to reach 400 saves.
  • 2007:Rivera finished the season with his career worst in earned runs (25), hits (68), and ERA (3.15) and his 30 saves in 34 opportunities were his second lowest total since 1998.
  • 2008:Before the start of the 2008 season, Rivera became a free agent and stated that he planned on testing the free agent market, however Rivera reamined with the Yankees by signing to a three-year, $45 million contract, which made him the highest-paid reliever in baseball history. Rivera rebounded from his worst career season by finishing his 2008 season with a 1.40 ERA with 39 saves in 40 opportunities, having one best seasons in his career.
  • 2009:Rivera reached another milestone by becoming the second pitcher with 500 regular season saves. He also recorded the final out during the 2009 World Series against the Philadelphia Phillies which was his fifth championship with the New York Yankees
  • 2010-2011:During the next two season, Rivera would continue dominate with his signature pitch "the cutter" and broke the record for the most saves in major league history, which was 602 saves.
  • 2012:During the 2012 season, Rivera suffered a freak injury when his right knee buckled on the field's warning while shagging balls during the pre-game causing him to tear his ACL, thus ending his season.
  • 2013:Before the start of the 2013 season Rivera signed a one-year, $10 million contract and announced that he would retire at the end of the season. Rivera finished his final major league season with a 2.11 ERA and 44 saves in 51 opportunities.
